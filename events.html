Inequalities and Technical Change, Labour Markets and Decarbonization Paths: Results from PRIN Projects

November 14, 2024 Multi-Institutional Research Conference
Participating Institutions: Università degli Studi di Milano-Bicocca, Ca' Foscari University of Venice, Sant'Anna School of Advanced Studies (Pisa), Università di Pavia

This comprehensive two-day interdisciplinary conference presents findings from four PRIN (Progetti di Rilevante Interesse Nazionale) research groups investigating the complex relationships between technological innovation, socioeconomic inequality, and environmental sustainability. The symposium addresses critical contemporary challenges in understanding how technological advancement influences labor market dynamics, income distribution patterns, and pathways toward decarbonization within modern economic systems.

Research Themes Addressed:

  • Technology-Inequality Nexus: Examination of how emerging technologies reshape income distribution and social stratification
  • Labor Market Transformation: Analysis of employment patterns, skill requirements, and workforce adaptation in response to technological change
  • Decarbonization Pathways: Investigation of sustainable technological transitions and their socioeconomic implications
  • Policy Integration: Development of frameworks for coordinating technological, social, and environmental policy objectives

Academic Significance: This conference represents a significant contribution to the emerging field of techno-social-environmental systems analysis, providing empirical evidence and theoretical frameworks for understanding the multidimensional impacts of technological change on contemporary society.

Shades of Hybrid: Hybrid Organizations and Networks Facing the Digital Challenge

June 26, 2024 Collaborative Research Workshop
Organizing Institutions: Università degli Studi di Milano-Bicocca, Ca' Foscari University of Venice, Sant'Anna School of Advanced Studies (Pisa), Università di Pavia
Collaborative Partners: BLISS Laboratory and YUNUS Laboratory (Ca' Foscari University)

This specialized workshop examines the evolving landscape of hybrid organizational forms within contemporary digital ecosystems. The event features presentations of Work Package (WP) deliverables developed through collaborative research initiatives with Ca' Foscari University's BLISS and YUNUS laboratories. The workshop addresses fundamental questions regarding how hybrid organizations—entities that combine elements from multiple institutional logics—navigate and adapt to digital transformation challenges.

Core Research Dimensions:

  • Organizational Hybridization: Analysis of multi-logic organizational forms and their adaptive mechanisms
  • Digital Transformation Impacts: Investigation of how digitalization reshapes hybrid organizational structures and networks
  • Network Dynamics: Examination of inter-organizational relationships within hybrid network configurations
  • Laboratory Collaboration: Integration of BLISS and YUNUS research methodologies for hybrid organization analysis

Theoretical Contribution: This workshop advances understanding of organizational hybridity within digital contexts, contributing to the theoretical development of neo-institutional theory and organizational ecology frameworks in contemporary business environments.
